Lebanon Cyber Training Exercises



Annual Simulated Cybersecurity Attack

  • Objective: Prepare Lebanonâ€™s cybersecurity professionals and government agencies for large-scale cyberattacks by implementing yearly cybersecurity training and practice through a simulated cyber attack.
  • Implementation:
    • Implement a nationwide created cyber crisis involving multiple sectors, including government and finance.
    • Test overall readiness and efficiency in solving the cybersecurity issue.
    • Test collaboration and crisis communication strategies.
  • Expected Outcome:
    • A more coordinated and faster national response to cyber threats.
    • Increased efficiency in problem-solving due to experience in real-world scenarios.
    • Identification of gaps in processes and procedures for continuous improvement.

Steps to Implement the Process

  • Form a multi-agency task force including representatives from government, finance, and critical infrastructure sectors.
  • Design a comprehensive simulated scenario based on real-world threats targeting Lebanon's national interests.
  • Develop clear roles and responsibilities for participants in the simulation.
  • Secure necessary tools, platforms, and infrastructure for the simulation.
  • Schedule regular training sessions and dry runs for all participants.
  • Execute the simulation and collect data on team performance and communication.
  • Conduct a post-simulation debrief to evaluate performance and identify areas for improvement.
  • Update procedures and protocols based on lessons learned from the simulation.
