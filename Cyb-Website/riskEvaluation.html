Risk Evaluation (Megan)


Risk 1: Fragmented Cybersecurity Landscape


Lebanon currently does not have a central cybersecurity agency and limited stakeholder cooperation, leaving the cyberspace unprepared for evolving threats (Lebanon National Cyber Security Strategy | Digital Watch Observatory, 2024)


Steps to Address this Risk:

  1. Create a national cybersecurity agency that will enforce regulations, manage incidents, and coordinate cooperation between public institutions, private companies, and international allies (Lebanon National Cyber Security Strategy | Digital Watch Observatory, 2024)
  2. Establish legal frameworks to protect critical infrastructure and enforce cybersecurity guidelines (Lebanon National Cyber Security Strategy | Digital Watch Observatory, 2024)


Risk 2: Lack of Skilled Cybersecurity Professionals

Lebanon faces a shortage of skilled cybersecurity professionals (Araz, 2019).


Steps to Address this Risk:

  1. Create educational programs at the high school and university level to train Lebanese citizens to become cybersecurity practitioners (Lebanon National Cyber Security Strategy | Digital Watch Observatory, 2024)
  2. Create a culture of collaboration between the government, academia, and private industry to help promote innovation and reduce the reliance on foreign technologies (Araz, 2019)
  3. Implement robust training initiatives for adults such as bootcamps and certification programs to make it easy for working people to pivot to a career in cybersecurity and increase their personal cybersecurity awareness (Lebanon National Cyber Security Strategy | Digital Watch Observatory, 2024)
