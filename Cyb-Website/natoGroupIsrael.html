  • Gaby Portnoy - Appointed to Israel National Cyber Director in 2022 - Age: 55 Years Old - Born in Argentina - Education: BS in Industrial Engineering from Shenkar College, MBA from Ono Academic College (2016) - Residence: Tel Aviv-Yafo, Tel Aviv District, Israel

Unit 8200 Cyber Skills and Specialization

  • Cyber Espionage: Implementation of intelligence through signals interception, cyber surveillance, and various monitoring techniques.
  • Cyber Operations: Known for critical cyber operations, including malware development and penetration of cyber infrastructure (e.g., STUXNET).
  • Signal Interception: High-level capabilities in intercepting and analyzing communications for intelligence.
  • Data and Signal Analysis: Analysis of intercepted signals to gain intelligence, such as phone and email interceptions to uncover a newly created Syrian nuclear reactor, and further exploitation through hacking of a Syrian laptop.
  • Technological Innovation: Many veterans contribute to Israel’s cybersecurity and tech industry, creating companies like Check Point, Palo Alto Networks, CyberArk, and SentinelOne.

Attacks Related to Unit 8200

AI Facial Recognition Attacks of the Israel Hamas War: Implemented in the Israel-Hamas conflict to track Hamas operatives within Israel, providing real-time intelligence for operational decisions.

  • Attack Summary: AI-based facial recognition systems used to enhance operational efficiency in conflict zones.
  • Reason for Attack: Intended to improve wartime decision-making efficiency, though it poses ethical challenges in civilian zones.
  • Ethical Issue: Inaccurate identification cases leading to escalated civilian casualties.
  • Global Implications: Sparked increased calls for stricter regulations on ethical AI use in military applications, highlighting potential war crimes.

Detection

  • Detecting Unit 8200 Activities: Deploy SIEM systems to identify unusual activities, unauthorized access attempts, and implement detailed system monitoring.
  • Intrusion Detection Systems: Apply these systems to monitor high-priority networks such as Industrial Control Systems, as Unit 8200 has been known to target nuclear facilities.
  • Network Segmentation: Isolate critical systems, monitoring ingress and egress points to secure sensitive communications.

Tracking Plan

  • Utilize Intelligence Networks: Collaborate with global intelligence-sharing platforms to stay informed on Unit 8200’s latest tactics and techniques.
  • Threat Hunting: Conduct regular threat-hunting exercises focused on Unit 8200’s expertise in signal interception and malware development.
  • Cyber Threat Knowledge and Training: Train personnel on Unit 8200’s techniques, such as signal interception and social engineering (e.g., phishing).
