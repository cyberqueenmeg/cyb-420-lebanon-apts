Lebanon Doctrine

Lebanon Cyber Laws

Cyberwarfare Doctrine

  • Defensive Measures: Focus on protecting critical infrastructure, government networks, and financial systems, often through international collaboration.
  • Offensive Capabilities: Indications of offensive capabilities exist, mainly for espionage or disruption.
  • Public Awareness and Resilience: Emphasis on increasing public awareness of cyber threats.

Cyber Laws and Regulations

1. Electronic Transactions Law (Law No. 81/2018)

Description: Regulates electronic transactions, digital signatures, and the use of electronic contracts.

Penalties: Violations can lead to fines and possible imprisonment, depending on severity.

2. Cybercrime Law (Law No. 105/2018)

Description: Addresses various cybercrimes, including hacking, data breaches, and online harassment.

Penalties: Fines (1 million to 10 million LBP) and imprisonment for up to 3 years.

NATO-EU Doctrine

European Union Cyber Laws

The Data Governance Act

The Data Governance act is a framework that creates structures to promote data sharing. It regulates international transfers of non-personal data, the reuse of public sector data, and a regime for data intermediaries. It also introduces data altruism organizations to collect and process data as well as the European Data Innovation Board.

Cyber Resilience Act

This proposed legislation introduces common cybersecurity rules for any item with digital components, including hardare and software.

(International Trade Administration, 2024)

US Doctrine

US Cyber Laws

Federal Cyber Laws

    Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) 1986

  • The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act is a federal law criminalizing unauthorized access to computer systems. It is one of the primary laws for prosecuting cybercrime within the United States. The Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 is applicable when foreign threat actors or nation states engage in either hacking or cyber espionage against the United States. Any attack on critical infrastructure like financial systems, energy grids, or water plants can be prosecuted using the CFAA.

    •     The Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act (CISA) 2015

  • The CISA is put in place to encourage the sharing of cybersecurity threat information between private sector and the federal government in order to protect critical infrastructure from the threat of cyberattacks. The CISA facilitates efforts between federal agencies and private companies to both identify and mitigate threats coming from foreign cyber threat actors. Because of this the overall cyber defense structure of the United States is increased over time making the United States a more secure country.

    • State Level Cyber Laws

        California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) 2020

  • The CCPA holds value to the scope of cybersecurity, while indirect, the CCPA holds companies accountable for protecting and using sensitive user data against malicious breaches which in turn can be used in cyberattacks against the state or the country. This law mandates that if a California citizen’s data is leaked it must be reported, this in turn encourages sophisticated cybersecurity measures by implementing strict data protection protocols and ensuring companies invest in advanced security systems to prevent breaches.

    •     New York SHIELD Act (2019)

  • The New York SHIELD Act is a state law used to Stop Hacks and Improve Electronic Data Security (SHIELD). This act is put in place to increase data security requirements for businesses operating within New York making it a requirement to have adequate cybersecurity measures put in place as well as having a notification response about any potential sensitive data loss. The SHIELD Act helps businesses and corporations’ preparedness, in the event of a cyber attack as well as increasing the overall cybersecurity structure of them.
