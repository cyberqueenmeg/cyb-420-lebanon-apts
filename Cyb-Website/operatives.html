Lebanon's Cyber Team

Head of Cyberwarfare

Lebanon's head of cyberwarfare remains unknown largely due to the country's relatively small size and limited resources in the realm of cyberspace. Unlike larger countries with more detailed cyber infrastructure and better established cybersecurity frameworks, Lebanon's cyber capabilities are still developing. Because of this, the knowledge of who the head of Cyberwarfare is able to remain unknown to the public.

Military-Government Cyber Subordinates

President of Lebanon Michel Aoun As the head of state, the president is the supreme commander of the Lebanese Armed Forces and ultimately oversees national defense and security. Although not directly involved in cyber operations, the presidentâ€™s leadership influences national policies, including cyber defense strategy.

Prime Minister of Lebanon Najib Mikati The Prime Minister heads the executive branch of the government. He has oversight of the Ministry of Defense and the Ministry of Interior and Municipalities, both of which contain divisions responsible for national security, including cybersecurity.

Minister of Defense Maurice Slim The Ministry of Defense is responsible for national defense, which includes military cybersecurity initiatives. The Ministry oversees the armed forces, where the Military Intelligence Directorate plays a role in cyber warfare and defense strategies

Commander of Lebanese Armed Forces General Joseph Aoun The commander leads the entire military, including the intelligence and cybersecurity units. Under his command, the Military Intelligence Directorate plays a key role in protecting military networks and critical infrastructure from cyber threats.

Military Intelligence Directorate Brigadier General Wissam Safi This directorate is responsible for intelligence gathering, including in cyberspace. It oversees cybersecurity operations within the military, focusing on the protection of military systems, counter-cyberespionage, and ensuring the integrity of communication systems.

Minister of Interior and Municipalities The General Directorate of General Security oversees national security matters, including surveillance and counterintelligence, which increasingly involve cyberspace activities. The General Security agency would be responsible for monitoring and addressing cyber threats that impact national security

Deputy Director-General The Deputy Director-General oversees critical divisions that deal with cybersecurity. These divisions include counterintelligence and the cybersecurity division, both key in combating cyber threats to the nation.
